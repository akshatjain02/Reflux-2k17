Reflux presents ‘Poster Presentation’ which provides an opportunity for the emerging generation to showcase their innovative ideas in the form of a self-explanatory poster and aims to boost the technological and communicative skills.

Collaborate your talent, abilities and research work and put them on a poster with your creative skills.

Both Undergraduate and Postgraduate students will be eligible to submit their work.

Here are the topics on which posters can be submitted.

Submissions for Screening round are open!

Please read the section below to know the details and participate.

Rules

There can be a maximum 2 members in a team.

Guidelines:

  • Only, participants with valid Identity cards/Bonafide Certificates of their respective institutes are eligible to participate.
  • The size of the poster must be 36' x 48'
  • We suggest that you place a reproduction of the abstract in the upper left side of the poster, and use the headings 'Introduction','Methods','Results' and 'Conclusions' to identify your poster layout.
  • Include an acknowledgment section, containing details of anyone who helped you conduct this project.

Judging Criteria:
The posters will be judged on the following basis:

  • Well planned & organized
  • Presentation skills
  • Quality charts & images
  • Good use of literature
  • Thoroughly explained visual materials (charts/graphs etc)
  • Note: Your effectiveness in addressing the question from the audience will also be judged.

Note: The decision of the judges and the organizers shall be final and abiding.

Online Screening round

  • In the screening round, you need to submit an abstract (brief summary) of the poster (should be less than 400 words)
  • The abstract has to be submitted in a Word or PDF file format and must follow the template given below
  • Last date for submitting the abstract is 21th March
  • The shorlisted teams(based on the abstract) will be declared in about a week after the last date of submission
  • The shorlisted teams will have to send a soft copy of their poster before 15th March to qualify for the onsite round

Note: The decision of the judges and the organizers shall be final and abiding.

Onsite round

  • A well designed poster based on the abstract needs to be presented in front of the judges.
  • Be sure to include the abstract title, names of author(s) and your institution in large letters at the top center of the poster.
  • Place your phone number and email address in the upper right-hand corner.
  • Keep your poster presentation to 15 minutes per visitor/judge.
  • Come prepared with any relevant handouts you may wish to share and business cards to hand out.
  • Participants will have to pay a registration fee of 300/- on the spot during the Onsite round.

Note: The decision of the judges and the organizers shall be final and abiding.
