Industrial Design Problem
About

"We keep moving forward, opening new doors, and doing new things, because we're curious and curiosity keeps leading us down new paths"-Walt Disney

Whether it's a mobile phone, a vacuum cleaner or a chair, the very best examples of industrial design seamlessly blend form and function to make products truly desirable. As its name implies, this challenge is about all balancing creative, conceptual, freeform thinking with the practical, industrial constraints of actually getting something made, which requires knowledge of production processes, materials and technology. So if you think you have all the above qualities then step into the premises of IDP to solve the most common engineering practicalities and come up with the most economical & feasible solution option for the problem.
